Research Team

Meet our dedicated researchers who are advancing knowledge in child and adolescent development

Katherine Reid

PhD Student in Developmental Psychology

Katherine completed her Master's in Developmental Psychology at Wilfrid Laurier University and her Undergraduate Honours in Neuroscience and Mental Health at Carleton University.

Her research focuses on parent-child shared book reading and early literacy instruction. She is particularly interested in how digital technologies can support or hinder early reading development.

Katherine has presented her work at several conferences including the Development 2022 Conference and the Canadian Psychological Association Annual Convention.

Avnee Sharma

PhD Student in Developmental Psychology

Avnee completed her BA Psychology and MA Developmental Psychology at Wilfrid Laurier University.

Her research focuses on understanding the impact of social media influencers on young people's social and emotional development. She is examining how parasocial relationships with online personalities affect adolescents' identity formation and well-being.

Avnee has co-authored several publications on digital media use and youth development, and has been involved in community outreach programs focused on digital literacy.

Alia Hussain

Master's Student in Developmental Psychology

Alia completed her undergraduate degree in psychology at the University of Western Ontario in 2020 and is currently a second-year Developmental Psychology Master's Student.

Her research focuses on youth's social-emotional development, developmental psychopathology, and factors influencing the development of youth with ADHD and autism. She is particularly interested in how digital interventions can support neurodivergent youth.

Alia has been involved in several research projects examining technology use among children with developmental differences and has presented her work at regional psychology conferences.

Alumni

Former lab members who continue to make an impact in the field

Dr. Jane Smith

Former PhD Student, now Assistant Professor

Jane completed her PhD in Developmental Psychology at Wilfrid Laurier University in 2021.

Her research focused on digital media use and its effects on adolescent social development. She is now an Assistant Professor at Western University where she continues her research on youth and technology.

During her time at the CARE Lab, Jane published 5 peer-reviewed articles and presented at numerous international conferences.
